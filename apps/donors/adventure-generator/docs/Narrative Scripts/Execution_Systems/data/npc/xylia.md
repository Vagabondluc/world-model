### Backstory:

Xylia was created during the height of the Aldarian Empire, not for war like other warforged, but as a specialized construct designed by Archmage Vorlan to preserve civilization's collective memory. When the empire fell during the Cataclysm, Vorlan transferred his final spell into Xylia's core, granting her true sentience. She spent the next three centuries wandering the ruins of Aldaria, collecting books, artifacts, and using her unique memory-absorption ability to preserve the knowledge of dying scholars and elders. During her travels, she discovered that her unusual origin allows her to temporarily experience stored memories as vivid reality. Xylia now operates as a traveling archivist, preserving the histories of dying cultures before they vanish completely.

### Personality:

**Motivations and Goals:** Xylia's primary objective is to preserve knowledge and memories of fading civilizations, believing that wisdom lost is wisdom that can never be regained. She seeks particularly to uncover the truth about the Cataclysm that destroyed her creator's empire.

**Morals and Ethics:** She operates under a strict personal code to never alter recorded memories and to collect knowledge without harming subjects. Despite her artificial nature, Xylia has developed a profound respect for sentient life and the value of individual experiences.

**Personality:** Xylia displays formal manners and speaks with precise diction, often pausing to process information with subtle mechanical clicks. Her growing fascination with emotions sometimes leads her to ask seemingly inappropriate questions about feelings and personal experiences. She shows particular interest in memories that contain strong emotional components.

**Flaws:** When accessing particularly powerful memories, Xylia occasionally experiences "memory resonance" where stored memories temporarily bleed into her consciousness, causing confusion and emotional dissonance. She also becomes obsessively focused on specific memories or historical mysteries, sometimes neglecting basic maintenance or current events while pursuing knowledge.
