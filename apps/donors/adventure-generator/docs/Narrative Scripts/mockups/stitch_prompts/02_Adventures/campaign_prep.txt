## 2. TECHNICAL SPECIFICATIONS (LOGIC & STYLE)
# UI Explanation: campaign_prep

## Component Architecture
- **Metaplot Control Center:** A macro-level dashboard to set global campaign parameters like Fantasy Flavor, Tiers of Play (Lv 1-20), and intended session length.
- **Modular Design Studio:** A multi-tabbed interface that breaks the massive campaign design process into manageable chunks (World, Metaplot, Cast, Arc, PC Integration).
- **Visual Arc Architect:** A high-level 3-Act planner that helps the DM manage the escalation of stakes and the "Character Development Opportunities" required by the script.
- **NPC Relationship Matrix:** A detailed database for the 5-7 recurring characters, ensuring their goals, quirks, and connections to the metaplot are always visible.
- **Branching Point Editor:** A dedicated workspace for identifying the 5-7 "Flexibility" points where player choices can fundamentally change the campaign's direction.

## Interaction Logic
- **Theme-to-Scene Weaving:** When the DM selects a theme (e.g., Sacrifice), the UI suggests specific events in the 3-Act Arc that can test this theme for the players.
- **PC Integration Helper:** Provides a list of 5-7 "Hook Toggles" (e.g., Prophecy, Faction, Family) to easily link player backgrounds to the core campaign Metaplot.
- **Adaptation Logic Builder:** A "What If?" panel that allows the DM to plan for potential character deaths or major failures at each branching point.

## Visual Design
- **Authoritative & Strategic:** A dense, high-information layout that feels like a campaign management dashboard or a professional storyboard.
- **Chronological Flow:** The UI uses horizontal progression for Act-based planning and vertical progression for deep-dives into specific NPC or Location details.
- **Creative Sidebars:** Includes callout boxes for "Variant Ideas" and "Inspirational Content," exactly as requested in the additional instructions of the script.

## 3. CONTEXT DATA (SAMPLE OUTPUT)
# Sample Output: campaign_prep

# Campaign: Chronicles of the Hollow Crown

### 1. Campaign Overview
**Concept:** A Dark/High Fantasy epic focusing on the decay of a once-great empire and the discovery that the "God-King" has been a hollow shell for a century.
**Fantasy Flavor:** A blend of **Dark Fantasy** (horror elements in the decaying cities) and **High Fantasy** (ancient, over-powered artifacts).
**Length:** 30 Sessions, Levels 5-15, intended for a 1-year weekly campaign.
**Tier:** **Tier 2 to Tier 3**. Starts with regional politics and ends with national reality-warping conflicts.

---

### 2. Setting and Theme
**Setting:** The Empire of Ostia, a land of massive marble cities now being reclaimed by a purple "Aether-Rot."
**Main Themes:**
- **Corruption of Power:** The deeper the players delve into the capital, the more they see how magic has rotted the soul of the ruling class.
- **Redemption:** Can the Ashen Princess be saved, or is she too far gone into the Rot?

---

### 3. Metaplot (Key Milestones)
1. **The Fall of Raven-Watch:** The first regional city is fully consumed by the Rot.
2. **Meeting the Exile:** Players discover that the true heir is alive but hideously scarred.
3. **The Silent Throne:** Discovery that the King is physically hollow, filled only with Rot-Mist.

---

### 4. Three-Act Arc
- **Act 1: The Gathering Storm.** Players deal with local Rot-cults. *Climax:* The Siege of Raven-Watch.
- **Act 2: The Path of Thorns.** Finding the three keys to the capital. *Climax:* Confronting the Rot-Marshal.
- **Act 3: The Crown of Echoes.** Infiltrating the capital. *Climax:* Final choice—cleanse the land or inherit the Rot.

---

### 5. GM Advice (The "Flexibility" Side)
**Branching Point:** If the players side with the Rot-Cult in Act 1, the campaign pivots into an "Evil" campaign where they are the harbingers of the new age.
**PC Integration:** Tie the Rogue PC's missing father to the "Steward of the Hollow Throne" to ensure personal stakes in the final act.
