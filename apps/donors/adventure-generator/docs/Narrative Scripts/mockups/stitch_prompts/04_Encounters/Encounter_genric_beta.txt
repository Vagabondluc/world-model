## 2. TECHNICAL SPECIFICATIONS (LOGIC & STYLE)
# UI Explanation: Encounter_genric_beta

## Component Architecture
- **Realm Themed Interface:** A visual selector for "Mystical Realms" that changes the overall UI aesthetic (e.g., starry astral background vs shadow-wrapped border).
- **Narrative Word-Count Console:** A central text area with a prominent word counter that changes color (Yellow -> Green -> Red) as the user approaches the "500-word" sweet spot.
- **Twin outcome Panels:** Dedicated fields for "Success Consequences" and "Failure Consequences", presented side-by-side to ensure balance.
- **NPC Profile Card:** A small, detailed block covering "Motive", "Goal", and "Cunning Tactics".

## Interaction Logic
- **Success-Based XP Calculations:** The XP fields are mathematically linked. If "Success [800]" is entered, "Failure" can be set as a percentage (e.g., 25%) or a manual value.
- **Sectioned Formatting:** The UI automatically injects section headers (e.g., "The Environment", "The Challenge") into the final export to ensure the "Methodical Arrangement" required by the script.
- **AI Inspiration Chips:** Suggests "Mystical Realms" or "Environmental Twists" based on a single seed word.

## Visual Design
- **High-Phantasy Aesthetic:** Uses glowing borders, serif typography, and translucent panels to evoke a "captivating story" feel.
- **Status Indicators:** Clear badges for "Environmental Mechanics" and "Combat Stats Reference" ensure the DM doesn't miss key rules during play.
- **Export Preview:** A stylized view showing how the content will look in a digital adventure log.

## 3. CONTEXT DATA (SAMPLE OUTPUT)
# Sample Output: Encounter_genric_beta

# Scene: The Prism-Plains (Feywild)

### The Environment: Mirror-Rain
A light, crystalline drizzle falls from a purple sky. Every drop acts as a tiny prism, reflecting and distorting the landscape.
- **Effect:** All visual perception is reflected. Creatures have disadvantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks relying on sight. 
- **Twist:** Any creature that takes a Long Rest in the rain must succeed on a DC 13 Charisma save or their reflection becomes their "dominant" self (purely roleplay change).

### The Narrative Focus: Elara the Shimmer
The party encounters Elara, a Dryad with bark like polished chrome. She is weaving a crown from "Glass-Flowers" that grow in the Mirror-Rain.
- **Goal:** Parley with Elara to gain passage through the Prism-Gate.
- **Challenge:** She asks three "Reflected Riddles" (riddles where the answer is the mirror-opposite of what it seems).
- **Skill Check:** DC 14 Charisma (Persuasion) or Intelligence (Investigation) to decipher her logic.

### Opponents: Guardian reflections
If the parley fails or Elara is threatened:
- **Statblock:** 2x "Mirror Images" (Use *Shadow* statblock but Change damage type to Psychic).
- **Tactics:** They mirror the players' movements, focusing on the PC with the highest AC first.
- **Motive:** Elara only wants to protect her Glass-Flowers; the reflections are purely defensive.

### XP Rewards
- **Total Success (Gate Opened):** 800 XP
- **Partial Success (Fled, but found path):** 400 XP
- **Failure (Captured/Forced retreat):** 200 XP

### Outcomes
- **Success:** Elara gives a Glass-Flower crown. **Consequence:** PCs ignore Mirror-Rain penalties for 24 hours.
- **Failure:** The Mirror-Rain intensifies. **Consequence:** The party wakes up 1 mile back, missing one non-magical item stolen by reflections.
