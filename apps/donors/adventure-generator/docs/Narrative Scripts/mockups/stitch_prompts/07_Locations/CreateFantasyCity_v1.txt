## 2. TECHNICAL SPECIFICATIONS (LOGIC & STYLE)
# UI Explanation: CreateFantasyCity_v1

## Component Architecture
- **Settlement Identity Panel:** Controls the core demographics and biome alignment. It ensures the "Dominant Race" logic flows through to shopkeepers and historical events.
- **Architectural Layer Manager:** Specifically handles the unique requirements of "Glade Circles" vs. "Treetop Housing." It includes visual toggles for rope bridges and natural resource utilization.
- **Cultural & Artistic Gallery:** A focused workspace for defining the "Soul" of the city through statues, gardens, and tapestries.
- **Lore & Succession Timeline:** Manages the history of the "Luminarch" and the city's military/political past (Battles and Coups).
- **Merchant Manifest Editor:** A strict validation tool that ensures all 10 required shop types are named and described before the project is marked "Done."

## Interaction Logic
- **Biome-Integrated Suggestions:** Changing the biome to "Dark Timberlands" automatically updates the suggested descriptions for architecture (e.g., using Ironbark or Shadow-pine).
- **History-to-Npc link:** Major historical events automatically generate relevant NPCs (e.g., a "Battle of the Spire" veteran or a descendant of the first Luminarch).
- **Auto-naming Registry:** Provides thematic name suggestions for shops based on the city's name and dominant race.

## Visual Design
- **Natural Heritage Aesthetic:** Uses earthy tones (Greens, Tans, Browns) and organic borders that feel like carved wood or parchment.
- **Hierarchical Layout:** The UI progresses from the macro (City Identity) to the micro (Specific Shops), mirroring the DM's creative process.
- **Iconography:** Uses symbols for trees, hammers (shops), and crowns (government) to categorize information.

## 3. CONTEXT DATA (SAMPLE OUTPUT)
# Sample Output: CreateFantasyCity_v1

# City Profile: Sylvaris, The Emerald Thicket

### 1. Population & Races
- **Total Population:** 8,200
- **Dominant Race:** **Wood Elves (85%)**. Native to the Dark Timberlands, they have lived in harmony with the great Iron-Oaks for millennia.
- **Minority Races:** Humans (10%), Halflings (5%).

### 2. Government
- **Leadership:** **The Luminarch Council**. A tripartite body consisting of the High Druid (Spiritual), the Wood-Marshal (Military), and the Chronicler (Law).
- **Luminarch History:** The current Luminarch, Thalessa Thorne, took power after the "Silent Coup" 50 years ago, shifting the city away from isolationism.

### 3. Architecture
- **The Central Circles:** Five concentric stone rings surrounding the **Primal Glade**, where the most sacred rituals occur.
- **Natural Housing:** Houses at the base of the Iron-Oaks are built from living wood and stone, merging seamlessly with the forest floor.
- **The Canopy District:** Elaborate treetop villas suspended 100ft in the air, connected by swaying rope bridges and carved wooden walkways.

### 4. Artwork & Culture
- **The Sentinel Statue:** A 30ft tall figure of the first Luminarch carved from a single petrified tree.
- **The Whisper Tapestries:** Massive woven hangings in the Council Hall that record the city's history in glowing silver thread.
- **The Gloom-Bloom Gardens:** Shimmers of bioluminescent flora that provide the city's primary light source at night.

### 5. History
- **The Founding:** Sylvaris was created 1,200 years ago as a sanctuary during the "War of Burning Skies."
- **The Siege of Thorns:** A failed invasion by the Orcish war-bands that resulted in the city's fortification with "Living Spikes."

### 6. The Marketplace (Local Shops)
1. **The Arcane Root (Magic Shop):** Sells wands grown rather than forged.
2. **Iron-Bark Bastion (Armorer):** Specializes in high-durability wood armor.
3. **The Leaf-Edge (Weaponsmith):** Forges "Dark-Glass" blades.
4. **The Herbalist’s Lungs (Herbalist):** Located in the highest tree-hollow.
5. **The Alchemist’s Cauldron (Alchemist):** Famed for their Fire-Resistant sap.
6. **The Mossy Mug (Tavern):** Built into a massive hollowed stump.
7. **The Silent Quill (Scribe):** Sells parchment made from fungus.
8. **The Weaver’s Knot (General Goods):** Specialized in climbing gear.
9. **The Resin Works (Carpenter):** Essential for treetop maintenance.
10. **The Stable-Root (Stables):** Mounts include Giant Elks and Forest Ponies.
