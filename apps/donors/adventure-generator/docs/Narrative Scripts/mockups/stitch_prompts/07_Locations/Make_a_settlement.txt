## 2. TECHNICAL SPECIFICATIONS (LOGIC & STYLE)
# Make_a_settlement.txt — UI Explanation & React Component Design

## Overview
Extended settlement generator with 9 sections (Quick_settlement + Everyday Life + Challenges/Conflicts). Emphasizes cultural detail and conflict hooks for campaign integration.

## Component Architecture

### Main Component: `ExtendedSettlementGenerator`
```typescript
interface ExtendedSettlementState {
  mode: 'procedural' | 'ai';
  name: string;
  type: SettlementType;
  civilizationLevel: CivilizationLevel;
  seed: string;
  aiContext: string;
  
  // Output section toggles
  outputSections: Section[];
  
  // Everyday Life configuration
  everydayLife: {
    includeTraditions: boolean;
    includeMores: boolean;
    includeOccupations: boolean;
    includeFestivals: boolean;
    depth: 'brief' | 'standard' | 'detailed';
  };
  
  // Challenges/Conflicts configuration
  conflicts: {
    internal: {
      politicalFactions: boolean;
      socialTensions: boolean;
      economicStruggles: boolean;
    };
    external: {
      military: string; // User-specified threat
      environmental: boolean;
      supernatural: boolean;
    };
    count: number; // 1-8
  };
  
  generatedData: ExtendedSettlementData | null;
  loading: boolean;
}
```

### Child Components
1. **ModeSelector** — Procedural vs AI
2. **BasicSettlementForm** — Name, type, civilization level, seed
3. **SectionChecklist** — 9 output section checkboxes
4. **EverydayLifeConfig** — Sub-toggles + depth selector
5. **ConflictsConfig** — Internal/external category checkboxes + count slider
6. **SettlementPreview** — Markdown preview with formatted sections

## Data Flow

### Procedural Mode

#### Everyday Life Generation
1. **Traditions**
   - Depth = brief: 1-2 sentences
   - Depth = standard: 1 paragraph
   - Depth = detailed: 2-3 paragraphs with specific examples
   - Tables: `local_traditions`, `customs`, `taboos`
   - Examples: market days, coming-of-age rituals, greetings

2. **Cultural Mores**
   - Social norms, values, behavioral expectations
   - Tables: `social_norms`, `values`, `etiquette`
   - Examples: eye contact rules, helmet removal protocol

3. **Common Occupations**
   - Procedural: generate occupation percentages based on settlement type
   - Village: 70% agriculture, 10% crafts, 5% military, 15% other
   - City: 15% agriculture, 30% crafts, 15% military, 20% merchants, 20% other
   - Output: "Stonecutters (15%), miners (20%), blacksmiths (8%)..."

4. **Festivals/Holidays**
   - Generate 2-6 festivals based on civilization level
   - Tables: `seasonal_festivals`, `religious_holidays`, `commemorative_events`
   - Include: name, date/frequency, description, special activities

#### Challenges & Conflicts Generation
1. **Internal Disputes**
   - Political: faction rivalries, succession disputes, corruption
   - Social: class tensions, racial prejudice, generational gaps
   - Economic: resource scarcity, guild disputes, wealth inequality
   - Generate 1-4 internal conflicts based on selected categories

2. **External Threats**
   - Military: user specifies (e.g., "Orc raiders", "Neighboring kingdom")
   - Environmental: natural disasters, resource depletion, plague
   - Supernatural: haunted locations, planar rifts, curses
   - Generate 1-4 external threats

3. **Conflict Output Format**
   ```markdown
   ## Challenges & Conflicts
   1. **Internal: Guild Dispute** — Miners' Guild and Merchants' Guild...
   2. **External: Orc Raids** — Monthly raids from Bloodfang clan...
   3. **Supernatural: Haunted Mine** — Northern silver mine abandoned...
   ```

### AI Mode
- Construct prompt with all enabled sections + configuration
- Example prompt segment:
  ```
  Include Everyday Life section with:
  - Local traditions (detailed depth)
  - Cultural mores
  - Common occupations with percentages
  - 3-4 festivals/holidays
  
  Include Challenges & Conflicts section with:
  - 1 political faction conflict
  - 1 social tension
  - External military threat: Orc raiders
  - 1 supernatural threat
  Total: 4 conflicts
  ```

## Tables (Procedural Mode)

### Everyday Life Tables
- `traditions`: 50+ local customs (market days, rituals, ceremonies)
- `mores`: 40+ social norms (greetings, respect signals, taboos)
- `occupation_templates`: 30+ job categories with default percentages per settlement type
- `festivals`: 30+ celebration templates (seasonal, religious, commemorative)

### Conflict Tables
- `internal_political`: 25+ faction disputes, power struggles
- `internal_social`: 20+ class/race/generational tensions
- `internal_economic`: 15+ resource/trade/guild conflicts
- `external_military`: 30+ enemy factions (orcs, bandits, rival nations)
- `external_environmental`: 20+ natural disasters, plagues, shortages
- `external_supernatural`: 25+ magical threats (curses, hauntings, planar rifts)

## Civilization Level Effects

| Level | Traditions | Festivals | Conflicts | Occupation Diversity |
|-------|----------|-----------|-----------|---------------------|
| Primitive | 1-2 | 1-2 | 1-2 | Low (5 types) |
| Basic | 2-3 | 2-3 | 2-3 | Medium (10 types) |
| Advanced | 3-5 | 4-6 | 3-5 | High (20+ types) |

## Validation Rules
- Settlement type: required
- Civilization level: required
- Everyday Life: at least 1 sub-toggle enabled if section enabled
- Conflicts: at least 1 internal OR external category selected if section enabled
- Conflict count: 1-8
- External military threat: text required if military checkbox selected

- Everyday Life sub-toggles: fieldset with legend

# STONEWATCH

## Population & Demographics

**Population:** 8,500

Primarily human (70%), dwarf (20%), halfling (8%), with small populations of gnomes and half-orcs (2%). Stonewatch is a militarized frontier city with high soldier-to-civilian ratio (25% military). Gender distribution is balanced (52% male, 48% female). Age demographics: 25% children, 65% adults, 10% elderly. High population turnover due to military rotations.

## History & Origins

Founded 200 years ago as a frontier fort during the **Border Wars** between the Kingdom of Valoria and the Orcish Clans. Initially a military outpost garrisoning 500 soldiers, Stonewatch grew as civilians (merchants, craftsmen, farmers) settled nearby to supply the garrison. The fort's success in repelling orc raids earned it city charter 150 years ago.

The city's name derives from the massive **Watchtower of the Sentinel**, a 100-foot stone tower visible for miles, built by dwarven engineers. The tower has never fallen, symbolizing Stonewatch's resilience.

## Economy & Resources

**Primary Industry:** Military support (25% of economy)

Stonewatch's economy revolves around supporting the garrison and defending the frontier. Major economic sectors:
- **Military Contracting:** Weapon forging, armor repair, siege equipment manufacturing
- **Mining:** Nearby iron and copper mines supply raw materials (20%)
- **Agriculture:** Outlying farms produce grain, livestock (15%)
- **Trade:** Caravan hub for frontier settlements (20%)
- **Services:** Inns, taverns, blacksmiths, mercenary companies (20%)

**Resources:** Iron ore, copper, timber, grain, livestock

**Trade Partners:** Capital city of Valoria (weapons exports), dwarven mountain holds (engineering contracts), frontier settlements (food/tool supplies)

## Government & Law

**Government Type:** Military Governor with Civilian Council

Stonewatch is governed by a **Military Governor** (appointed by the king, 5-year term) who holds executive authority over defense and foreign policy. A **Civilian Council** of 5 elected representatives handles domestic matters (taxes, infrastructure, civil disputes).

**Current Governor:** General Marcus Ironwall (human, 52), veteran of 30 years' service. Strict but fair. Prioritizes defense over economic growth.

**Laws:** Military law applies to garrison personnel; civilian law applies to residents. Martial law can be declared during emergencies (requires council approval after 72 hours). Weapons must be peace-bonded within city limits. Curfew (midnight-dawn) enforced during heightened alert.

## Key Locations

1. **The Watchtower of the Sentinel** — 100-foot stone tower at city center. Garrison command post, signal beacon, and symbolic heart of city. Houses 50 elite guards.

2. **The Iron Barracks** — Military complex housing 2,000 soldiers. Training grounds, armory, infirmary, mess halls. Civilians restricted unless authorized.

3. **The Forge Quarter** — District of 30+ blacksmith shops specializing in military equipment. Air thick with smoke and hammer strikes. Artisans' Guild controls quality standards.

4. **The Market Square** — Central plaza with daily market. 80+ vendor stalls sell food, tools, weapons, armor. Mercenary hiring office located here.

5. **The Wall** — 25-foot stone fortifications encircling city. 8 watchtowers, 4 gates (North, South, East, West). Patrolled 24/7 by garrison.

6. **The Memorial Gardens** — Park honoring fallen soldiers. 1,500+ names carved in marble monuments. Annual Remembrance Day ceremony held here.

## Everyday Life

### Traditions

**Weekly Market Day:** Every Seventhday, the Market Square hosts expanded market with farmers, traders, and entertainers from outlying settlements. Citywide social event where news is exchanged and deals are made.

**Bell Tower Chimes:** The Watchtower bell chimes at dawn and dusk, marking work hours. Civilians adjust schedules to these chimes. Military drills occur between dawn and noon daily.

**Apprentice Sashes:** Apprentices in all trades wear colored sashes indicating their guild (red = blacksmiths, blue = masons, green = merchants, etc.). Tradition started 100 years ago to identify skilled labor during city expansion.

**Veterans' Privileges:** Retired soldiers receive lifetime discounts (10-20%) at most businesses. Veterans given priority seating in taverns and public forums. Cultural norm honoring military service.

### Cultural Mores

**Direct Eye Contact:** Business transactions require direct eye contact as sign of honesty. Avoiding eye contact suggests deception or cowardice. Foreigners often offend locals unknowingly.

**Helmet Removal Indoors:** Removing one's helmet when entering buildings (especially homes, taverns, temples) is sign of respect and peace. Keeping helmet on implies hostility or distrust.

**Military Rank Deference:** Civilians defer to military officers in public spaces (step aside on streets, speak respectfully). Not legally required but socially expected. Violating norm risks social ostracism.

**Shield Oath:** Swearing "by my shield" is binding verbal contract. Breaking shield oath results in social disgrace and potential exile. Tradition rooted in military honor code.

### Common Occupations

- **Soldiers/Guards:** 25% (2,125 people)
- **Miners:** 20% (1,700 people)
- **Blacksmiths/Artisans:** 15% (1,275 people)
- **Merchants/Traders:** 12% (1,020 people)
- **Farmers (within city limits):** 8% (680 people)
- **Innkeepers/Tavern Owners:** 5% (425 people)
- **Laborers/Servants:** 10% (850 people)
- **Other (healers, scribes, priests, etc.):** 5% (425 people)

### Festivals & Holidays

1. **Remembrance Day** (Spring): Honors fallen soldiers. Solemn ceremony at Memorial Gardens. Veterans parade through streets. City-wide moment of silence at noon.

2. **Founder's Feast** (Summer): Celebrates city charter anniversary. Three-day festival with military demonstrations, blacksmithing competitions, archery contests, feasting.

3. **Harvest Festival** (Autumn): Celebrates agricultural yields. Farmers' markets, livestock shows, communal feast. Proceeds fund city orphanage.

4. **Winterwatch** (Winter Solstice): Week-long vigilance period honoring the Watchtower's unbroken defense. Extra patrols, torch-lighting ceremonies, storytelling of historical battles.

## Challenges & Conflicts

1. **Internal: Guild Dispute**  
   The Miners' Guild and Merchants' Guild are clashing over export pricing. Miners demand higher ore prices (claiming dangerous work justifies premium), while merchants want lower costs to stay competitive with capital city suppliers. Civilian Council deadlocked 2-2 (5th councilor abstaining due to family ties to both guilds). Tensions rising, strike threatened.

2. **External: Orc Raids**  
   Monthly raids from **Bloodfang Clan** targeting outlying farms and mining outposts. Raiders strike at night, steal livestock, burn crops. City walls protect urban core, but farmlands suffer. Garrison patrols reduced food production by 30% (farmers fleeing to safety of city). Governor Ironwall offering 500gp bounty on orc chieftain's head.

3. **Supernatural: Haunted Mine**  
   Northern silver mine (Shaft 7, oldest section) abandoned after workers reported ghostly figures and phantom voices. Production down 30%, Miners' Guild refuses to enter. Governor hired adventurers to investigate, but first party never returned. Rumors of ancient dwarven burial ground disturbed by mining operations.

4. **Internal: Succession Crisis**  
   Governor Ironwall's 5-year term expires in 6 months. King has not announced reappointment or replacement. Rival factions forming: Military faction supports Ironwall's reappointment; Merchant faction wants civilian governor (Councilor Stoneheart). Political maneuvering intensifying.

## Hooks & Adventure Seeds

1. **Bounty on the Chieftain:** PCs hired to hunt Bloodfang Clan chieftain. 500gp reward + military honors. Leads to orc stronghold in Thornwood Forest, potential treaty negotiation instead of assassination.

2. **The Haunted Mine Mystery:** Miners' Guild offers 200gp to investigate Shaft 7. Truth: Ancient dwarven vault contains cursed artifact. Removing curse requires quest to recover lost relic.

3. **Guild War Mediation:** Civilian Council hires PCs as neutral mediators. Resolution requires uncovering corruption (5th councilor secretly invested in capital city merchants, sabotaging local guild negotiations).

4. **The Succession Plot:** One of the political factions approaches PCs to dig up dirt on rival candidate. Investigation uncovers assassination plot targeting Governor Ironwall.

5. **Refugee Crisis:** Neighboring frontier settlement destroyed by orc horde. 300 refugees arrive at Stonewatch gates. City resources strained. PCs must secure food supplies, prevent refugee-resident conflicts, and gather intelligence on orc movements.
