## 2. TECHNICAL SPECIFICATIONS (LOGIC & STYLE)
# UI Explanation: Quick_settlement

## Component Architecture
- **Image-First Dashboard:** A unique interface that leads with visual inspiration (DALL-E) before diving into narrative text. It helps set the tone for the rest of the generation process.
- **Priority Input Module:** Captures the "UTMOST PRIORITY" elements early, ensuring the backend AI weighs these more heavily than standard tropes.
- **Sectioned Narrative Blocks:** Organizes the 10+ core domains (Governance, Economy, Customs, etc.) into clean, tabbed containers, each labeled with its expected style (e.g., [ND, D&T]).
- **Relational NPC Table:** A structured grid for "Prominent Individuals" that forces the user (and AI) to define specific "Goals" for every character, creating immediate plot momentum.
- **Multi-Vector Hook Builder:** A specialized tool to draft distinct types of adventures (Mysteries, Political Intrigue, Moral Dilemmas) that are cross-referenced with the settlement's conflicts.

## Interaction Logic
- **Visual-to-Text Consistency:** The UI allows the user to click "Sync" to ensure that architectural details described in the text match the visual features visible in the generated DALL-E image.
- **Automatic Table Expansion:** As the AI generates the "Society and Demographics" section, it automatically proposes entries for the NPC table (Leaders, Traders, etc.).
- **Style Enforcer:** The backend automatically applies the specific creative codes (C&S, D&T, ND) to the resulting text based on the section currently being edited.

## Visual Design
- **Sleek & Informative:** A professional, split-screen layout that balances rich visual media on the left with structured data on the right.
- **Role-Based Row Formatting:** In the NPC table, different colors or icons distinguish between "Political Leaders," "Heroes/Villains," and "Traders."
- **Code Badging:** Small, unobtrusive badges for style requirements (MS&L, AC&OT) provide at-a-glance status of the creative direction.

# Settlement Profile: Aethervale (The Sky-Isles)

![DALL-E Concept Image: A breathtaking floating city made of white marble and gold, suspended amidst colossal cumulonimbus clouds, with lightning-collecting spires and flying gondolas.](/docs/mockups/samples/aethervale-image.png)

## Settlement Overview
**Name:** Aethervale  
**Location:** The Thundering Reach  
**Pop:** 12,000 (Primarily Aarakocra and High Elves)

Aethervale is a literal "Cloud-City" suspended 15,000 feet above the Eternal Sea. It was founded by the Storm-Mages of the First Age to escape the Great Flood. Today, it is an international hub of magipunk innovation, providing 60% of the continent's "Stored-Lightning" energy. The city operates as a technocratic republic where access to fresh air and buoyancy stones is the primary form of wealth.

## Geography and Layout
The city is built on three massive floating discs of enchanted obsidian. **The High Reach** houses the labs and government; **The Cloud-Docks** handle trade; **The Under-Rim** is the industrial scrap-yard where the poor harvest lightning-mites. 

*Summary:* The city is a vertical marvel connected by sky-bridges and gravity-lifts. Defensive measures include "Storm-Cannons" that can focus lightning into devastating beams. Transportation is dominated by "Aether-Skiffs" and giant owls.

## Prominent Individuals (NPC Matrix)

| Name | Role | Description | Goal |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Luminarch Zephyr** | Political Leader | A silver-feathered Aarakocra with a prosthetic wing. | Maintain the city's buoyancy gems. |
| **Kaelen Voss** | Local Villain | A disgraced mage-engineer who lives in the rim. | Sabotage the Storm-Pylons to steal power. |
| **Sylvie Sun-Eye** | Noteworthy Trader | Captain of the *Solar-Strider* merchant fleet. | Monopoly on rare "Sun-Silk" imports. |
| **Balthazar** | Faith Leader | High Priest of the Thunder-God, Kord. | Predict the arrival of the "Great Maelstrom." |

## Adventure Hooks
- **The Dead Current (Mystery):** The gravity-lifts in the High Reach have stopped working. Is it a malfunction or a deliberate sabotage?
- **The Moral Dilemma:** A group of surface dwellers has arrived on a makeshift raft-ship. The High Reach refuses to let them up. Do you help them smuggle in, knowing it will strain the city's oxygen supply?

## GM Advice
Focus on the **Leitmotif of Height**. Every encounter should feel like it happens on a precipice. Use falling as a mechanical threat, but also use the "Aether-Steam" clouds to limit visibility for tactical combat.
