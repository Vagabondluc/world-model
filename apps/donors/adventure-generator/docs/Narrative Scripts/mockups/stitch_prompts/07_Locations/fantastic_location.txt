## 2. TECHNICAL SPECIFICATIONS (LOGIC & STYLE)
# UI Explanation: fantastic_location

## Component Architecture
- **Evocative Naming Tool:** A high-impact title generator that ensures names are brief (3-5 words) but descriptive.
- **Triptych Aspect Editor:** Specifically limits the user to "Exactly Three" aspects. This design choice prevents "Feature Creep" and forces focus on the three most interactive elements of the scene.
- **Sensory Palette Modal:** An interactive checklist for the 5 senses. It requires descriptions for at least three to ensure the scene feels "Alive."
- **Scale & Chronology Dashboard:** A simple, illustrative workspace for defining the physical dimensions and the primordial age of the location.
- **Background Context Mapper:** A logic-builder that links the location's lore to common RPG character archetypes (Archeology, Religious training, Arcane knowledge).
- **Concise Narrative Preview:** A real-time word-counter and text-block previewer to help the DM stay within the 300-400 word limit.

## Interaction Logic
- **Aspect-Driven Prompts:** Each aspect entered by the user generates potential "Interaction" suggestions (e.g., entering "Cracked Geode" might suggest "Mining for crystals" or "Magical resonance check").
- **Sensory Re-skinning:** Changing the theme of the location (from "Bone" to "Crystal") automatically updates the suggested sensory vocabulary.
- **Export to Scene Card:** Converts the dossier into a compact, printable format designed for a DM's binder or VTT macro.

## Visual Design
- **Cinematic & Moody:** Uses high-contrast typography and plenty of whitespace to emphasize the "Fantastic" nature of the content.
- **Focus on Imagery:** Icons for the 5 senses (Ear, Eye, Nose, Hand, Tongue) provide clear visual cues for narrative preparation.
- **Pacing HUD:** The word counter at the bottom acts as a "Pacing Guide," encouraging brevity and punchy descriptions.

## 3. CONTEXT DATA (SAMPLE OUTPUT)
# Sample Output: fantastic_location

# Location: The Bridge of Teeth

### 1. Aspects
- **Massive saber-toothed bestial skull** bursting out of the canyon wall.
- **Titan-sized ribs** tied together with cracked, splitting leather and sinew.
- **Deep hole in the jaw** like an infected wound that glows with a faint green light.

### 2. Scale and Age
- **Scale:** A 100-foot-long skeletal span crossing an 800-foot-deep chasm.
- **Age:** The bone is petrified and primordial, predating the rise of the first mortal empires by ten thousand years.

### 3. Sensory Details
- **Sight:** The bone is a sickly, weathered yellow, etched with silver glyphs that pulse with an erratic rhythm.
- **Sound:** The howling wind through the hollow ribs sounds like a chorus of moaning titan-ghosts.
- **Smell:** A sharp, metallic tang of ancient copper mixed with the stale dust of a billion years.

### 4. Potential Interactions
- **The Jaw-Mechanism:** Characters can attempt to "Unlock" the jaw to reveal a hidden passage (DC 17 Athletics or Arcana).
- **The Sinew Ropes:** Brave climbers can harvest the primordial sinew, though the bridge groans and shifts with every cut.

### 5. Hidden Secrets or Lore
- **Nexus of Energy:** Ancient texts refer to this skull as the "Anchor of the Abyss," a location where the boundary between planes is thin.
- **The Missing Fang:** One of the massive teeth is a disguised stone door leading to a hermit's cell.

### 6. Character Background Connections
- **Archeology:** A character with this background can identify that the sinew is made of a creature long extinct, confirming the bridge's impossible age.
- **Arcane:** A mage can sense that the green glow from the "wound" is a slow-leaking transmutation spell of epic proportions.
