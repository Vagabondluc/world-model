"The silvered light of Selûne bled through the cracks of the mountain, illuminating dust motes that danced like tiny, forgotten spells. "Elminster had warned him of the Seven Keys, but warned him in that maddeningly oblique way of Wizards—all riddles and pipe-smoke. The first key, the Sage of Shadowdale had claimed, was not forged of iron or gold, but of a memory of a scent of a rose that never bloomed in the Realms."

"As they approached the great obsidian archway of the Shadowdark, the air grew colder than a banshee's breath. Here, the laws of Mystra felt thin, frayed like an old tapestry left in a damp cellar.
