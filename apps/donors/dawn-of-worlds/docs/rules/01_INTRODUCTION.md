## The Experiment of Creation
Perhaps the most difficult prospect faced by fantasy game masters and authors is inventing a world from scratch. Dawn of Worlds offers a solution: create the world together.

Rather than a single author, a group of players become "gods" for an evening (about six hours) to raise up a world from foundations to civilizations, history, and politics.

## I. Gods
You and your friends gather around a table with a large sheet of paper (the world map). One player draws a rough outline of a land mass. Then, each player takes turns playing "god" to fill this barren world.

**Key Concepts:**
1.  **Shared Creation**: As you create things, other players are free to change or destroy them. This drives conflict and history.
2.  **Power Points**: Every turn, you roll dice to gain "power," which you spend to shape the land, create races, or command armies.
3.  **The Goal**: To create a fun, interesting world. You cannot "win," and no one can lose.
4.  **Disagreements**: If you cannot agree, flip a coin or vote.
