# II. Creation Process

Creation takes place in **Rounds**, grouped into three distinct **Ages**.

## The Round Structure
1.  **Power Roll**: At the start of each round, every player rolls **2d6** and adds the total to their current **Power Level**.
2.  **Turn Order**: Established at the start (usually highest roll goes first).
3.  **Actions**: Players spend points to take actions (shape land, create race, etc.).
4.  **Saving Points**: Players can choose to save points for expensive actions later.

## The Three Ages
Each Age focuses on a specific aspect of creation and lasts a minimum of **5 rounds**.

| Age | Focus | Duration | Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1st Age** | **Geography** | ~500 years/round | Shaping the physical world. Terrain is cheap; life is expensive. |
| **2nd Age** | **Races** | ~100 years/round | Populating the world. Races and cities appear. |
| **3rd Age** | **Relations** | ~Years/round | Politics, wars, and organizations. Civilizations interact and clash. |

**Progression**: After 5 rounds, players can vote to advance to the next Age. After 10 rounds, any single player can force an advance.

### 1st Age: Geography
*   **Focus**: Shaping the physical world.
*   **Common Actions**: Making mountains, rivers, forests, islands.
*   **Difficulty**: Creating races is "extremely difficult" (expensive) here.

### 2nd Age: Races
*   **Focus**: Raising fantasy races.
*   **Common Actions**: Creating Elves, Dwarves, Humans, etc., and their first cities.
*   **Difficulty**: Shaping land becomes more expensive.

### 3rd Age: Relations
*   **Focus**: Politics, war, and learning.
*   **Common Actions**: Wars, alliances, corruption/purification of cities, great leaders (Avatars).
*   **Difficulty**: Shaping land is most expensive here.
