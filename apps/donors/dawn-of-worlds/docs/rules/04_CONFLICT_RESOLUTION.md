# IV. Conflict Resolution

Conflicts are inevitable as cities raise armies and Avatars lure civilizations to darkness.

## Battle Mechanics
When armies engage, the outcome is decided by dice.

1.  **Bonuses**: Assign **+1** for each relevant advantage (Technology, High Ground, Walls, specialized terrain).
2.  **The Roll**: Each side rolls **2d6** + Bonuses.
3.  **Result**: High roll wins. The loser is destroyed or scattered.

### Multiple Battles
If an army fights multiple times in one turn (e.g., defeats Army A, then fights Army B):
*   **Fatigue Penalty**: Cumulative **-1 penalty** for each battle after the first.

## Wiping Things Out
It is difficult to completely exterminate something in Dawn of Worlds. Armies scatter, cities leave ruins, races hide.

*   **Cost**: To *completely* wipe out a fixture (City, Race, Order), you must pay its **equivalent Creation Cost** for the current Age.
    *   *Example*: Destroying a City in Age 1 costs **8 points** (same as creating it).
*   **Philosophy**: Wiping things out entirely makes the world less diverse. Only do it if it makes for a good story.
