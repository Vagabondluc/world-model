1.0 THEORETICAL FRAMEWORK
This gazetteer moves beyond traditional 2D map-making by introducing two additional axes of
exploration: Verticality (Strata) and Resonance (Spirit).
The Z-Axis (Strata): Recognizing that a fantasy world is not just a surface. Adventures
occur in the sky (Aero), on the ground (Terra), inside the crust (Litho), and in the crushing
depths of the planet's roots (Abyssal).
The W-Axis (Spirit): Recognizing that "biome" includes metaphysical laws. A forest in the
Material Plane follows biology; a forest in the Feywild follows emotion; a forest in the
Shadowfell follows entropy.
ZONE I: TROPICAL RAINFOREST
Whittaker Coordinates: High Temperature / High Precipitation Ecological Baseline: High
biomass, rapid decay, aggressive competition, vertical canopy layers. The struggle for sunlight
and nutrients drives hyper-evolution.
1. The Material Plane (Neutral)
Aero-Strata (Sky): The Thunder-Reaches
Concept: A permanent, localized hurricane system containing stable "eyes" where lush
jungle archipelagos float. The air pressure is heavy and humid, smelling of ozone and
crushed orchids.
Trope: Laputa / Skyward Sword.
Ecological Dynamics: Rain falls upward here due to massive thermal updrafts from the
surface. The islands are held aloft by magnetic lodestone cores.
Inhabitants:Aarakocra tribes who weave nests into the exposed root systems to avoid
the winds; Storm Giants who build fortresses of solidified lightning glass; Blue
Dragons that swim through the thunderheads like sharks.
Hazard:"Static-Burn." Metal armor attracts varying polarity charges, dealing
electrical damage if not grounded.

Concept: A high-pressure subterranean ocean of biological sludge, RNA-chains, and
proto-life. It is the womb of monsters.
Trope: The Ooze / Evolution Vats.
Ecological Dynamics: The fluid is not water, but a nutrient-rich amniotic gel. Creatures
do not breed here; they spontaneously generate from the muck based on the ambient
psychic stress of the world above.
Hazard:Biomass Dissolution. Non-magical organic gear (leather, wood, cloth)
dissolves into sludge after 1 hour of exposure.

ZONE II: SUBTROPICAL DESERT
Whittaker Coordinates: High Temperature / Low Precipitation Ecological Baseline: Water
scarcity, extreme heat, nocturnal activity, preservation. Life focuses on retention and shielding.
1. The Material Plane (Neutral)
Aero-Strata (Sky): The Sun-Scorched Mesas
Concept: Massive sandstone plateaus floating above the cloud layer, exposed to raw,
unfiltered sunlight. Water is the only currency that matters.
Trope: Dune (Airborne) / Treasure Planet.
Dynamics: Skyships use "Solar Sails" to catch light-pressure and thermal currents. The
shade of the islands is the only habitable zone.
Inhabitants:Wyverns nesting in canyon walls; Rocs hunting sky-whales; Nomadic
Sky-Pirates raiding for water tanks.
Survival: Dehydration checks are made every 4 hours instead of every day.
Terra-Strata (Surface): The Dune Sea
Concept: An ocean of sand, waves of heat, and buried civilizations. The wind creates
shifting geography, revealing and burying ruins daily.
Trope: Arabian Nights / The Mummy / Mad Max.
Resource:Ancient Artifacts. The dryness preserves paper, cloth, and bodies
perfectly for millennia.
Inhabitants:Giant Scorpions with crystal carapaces; Thri-Kreen hunters; Blue
Dragons that burrow through sand like water.
Hazard:Sandstorms. Reduce visibility to zero and deal flensing damage (1d6
slashing/round).
Litho-Strata (Upper Dark): The Magma Forge
Concept: Volcanic lava tubes and massive magma lakes. The heat is dry and searing.
The rock is black obsidian and basalt.
Trope: Mt. Doom / Ironforge / Blackrock Depths.
Inhabitants:Fire Giants crafting weapons of war; Salamanders swimming in the lava;
Azer building brass fortresses.
Ecological Dynamics: Creatures eat rocks and minerals (lithovores). Heat is life; cold is
death.
Hazard:Toxic Fumes. Clouds of sulfur require Constitution saves to avoid poisoning.
