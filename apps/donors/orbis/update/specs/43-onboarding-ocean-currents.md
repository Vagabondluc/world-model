# Ocean Currents Viewer Onboarding Guide

## Overview

The Ocean Currents Viewer provides detailed information about ocean circulation, upwelling zones, and marine productivity on your planet. It helps you understand how oceans affect climate and support marine life.

## Learning Objectives

### 🟢 Beginner (Ages 12-15)
- Understand what the Ocean Currents Viewer shows
- Learn to read current vector arrows
- Identify upwelling zones and marine productivity
- Understand how oceans affect climate

### 🟡 Intermediate (Ages 16-18)
- Understand ocean circulation patterns
- Interpret upwelling and productivity zones
- Forecast coastal impacts
- Use the viewer for ocean analysis

### 🔴 Advanced (Ages 19-99)
- Understand the ocean current snapshot system
- Interpret complex ocean circulation
- Use the viewer for climate modeling
- Understand the relationship between oceans and other domains

## Step-by-Step Walkthrough

### Step 1: Opening the Ocean Currents Viewer

1. From the main dashboard, click on **Ocean Currents Viewer** in the navigation menu
2. The viewer will load with a snapshot of current ocean conditions
3. You'll see three main sections:
   - **Current Map** (top): Vector field showing ocean current direction and speed
   - **Productivity Zones** (middle): Upwelling and marine productivity areas
   - **Action Panel** (bottom): Commands and projections

### Step 2: Reading Current Vectors

**🟢 Beginner:**

Current vectors show ocean current direction and speed:

| Arrow Direction | Meaning | Typical Speed |
|-----------------|---------|---------------|
| → Eastward | Current flowing east | 0.1-2 m/s |
| ← Westward | Current flowing west | 0.1-2 m/s |
| ↑ Northward | Current flowing north | 0.1-2 m/s |
| ↓ Southward | Current flowing south | 0.1-2 m/s |

**🟡 Intermediate:**

Each current vector shows:
- Direction (arrow points where water is going)
- Speed (arrow length and color)
- Depth (which layer of the ocean)
- Time (current or historical)

**🔴 Advanced:**

Current vectors are computed from:
- Wind stress on surface
- Density differences
- Coriolis effect
- Topographic steering

### Step 3: Understanding Upwelling Zones

**🟢 Beginner:**

Upwelling zones bring deep, nutrient-rich water to the surface:

```
Surface Water  ◄───────────────────────
               (cold, nutrient-rich)
Deep Water     ───────────────────────►
               (warm, nutrient-poor)
```

**🟢 Beginner:**

Upwelling zones are important because:
- They bring nutrients from deep water
- Nutrients feed phytoplankton
- Phytoplankton feed fish and other marine life
- Many fisheries are located near upwelling zones

**🟡 Intermediate:**

Upwelling zones show:
- Location (where upwelling occurs)
- Strength (how much water rises)
- Seasonality (when upwelling is strongest)
- Productivity (how much life it supports)

**🔴 Advanced:**

Upwelling is caused by:
- Wind pushing surface water away from coast
- Ekman transport
- Density differences
- Topographic effects

### Step 4: Understanding Marine Productivity

**🟢 Beginner:**

Marine productivity shows how much life the ocean can support:

| Productivity Level | Color | Meaning |
|-------------------|-------|---------|
| High | Dark Green | Lots of phytoplankton and fish |
| Medium | Light Green | Moderate marine life |
| Low | Blue | Little marine life |

**🟡 Intermediate:**

Marine productivity depends on:
- Nutrient availability (from upwelling)
- Sunlight (for photosynthesis)
- Water temperature
- Seasonal changes

**🔴 Advanced:**

Productivity is measured by:
- Chlorophyll concentration
- Phytoplankton biomass
- Primary production rates
- Trophic level estimates

### Step 5: Inspecting Current Bands

**🟢 Beginner:**

See ocean currents in a specific area:
1. Click on any hex on the ocean map
2. View current details in the inspector panel
3. See direction, speed, and depth
4. Compare to surrounding cells

**🟡 Intermediate:**

Current band inspection shows:
- Current vector components (u, v, w)
- Speed and direction
- Depth profile
- Temperature and salinity

**🔴 Advanced:**

Current band data includes:
- Full 3D current vector
- Turbulence metrics
- Shear calculations
- Water mass properties

### Step 6: Showing Anoxia Risk

**🟢 Beginner:**

See where ocean water might lose oxygen:
1. Click **"Show Anoxia Risk"**
2. View areas at risk for low oxygen
3. Understand why anoxia is dangerous
4. See how it might change over time

**🟡 Intermediate:**

Anoxia risk shows:
- Oxygen concentration levels
- Areas below critical thresholds
- Historical trends
- Future projections

**🔴 Advanced:**

Anoxia is caused by:
- Stratification (layers not mixing)
- High nutrient loading
- Warming temperatures
- Low circulation

### Step 7: Forecasting Coastal Impacts

**🟢 Beginner:**

See how ocean changes affect coasts:
1. Click **"Forecast Coastal Impacts"**
2. Choose a time horizon (10, 50, 100 years)
3. View projected coastal changes
4. Compare to current conditions

**🟡 Intermediate:**

Coastal impact forecasts show:
- Sea level rise
- Erosion risk
- Storm surge changes
- Fisheries impacts

**🔴 Advanced:**

Coastal impact forecasts use:
- Sea level projections
- Wave climate models
- Sediment transport
- Ecosystem models

## Key Concepts

### 🟢 Beginner Concepts

1. **Ocean Current** - Movement of ocean water in a specific direction
2. **Upwelling** - Deep, nutrient-rich water rising to the surface
3. **Marine Productivity** - How much life the ocean can support
4. **Anoxia** - Water with very little or no oxygen

### 🟡 Intermediate Concepts

1. **Ocean Circulation** - Large-scale movement of ocean water
2. **Ekman Transport** - How wind moves surface water
3. **Stratification** - Layers of water with different properties
4. **Primary Production** - Phytoplankton growth from sunlight

### 🔴 Advanced Concepts

1. **Ocean Current Snapshot** - Complete ocean state at one tick
2. **Water Mass** - Water with specific temperature and salinity
3. **Thermohaline Circulation** - Deep ocean circulation driven by density
4. **Trophic Level** - Position in the food chain

## Troubleshooting

### Issue: Current vectors show "Loading..."

**Solution:**
- Check if the simulation is running
- Verify ocean current is computed
- Try refreshing the viewer

### Issue: Productivity zones are not visible

**Solution:**
- Check if phytoplankton data is available
- Verify productivity overlay is active
- Adjust visualization settings

### Issue: Anoxia risk shows no data

**Solution:**
- Check if oxygen is tracked in the simulation
- Verify ocean depth is sufficient
- Review error message for specific issue
