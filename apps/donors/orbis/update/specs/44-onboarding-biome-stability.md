# Biome Stability Atlas Onboarding Guide

## Overview

The Biome Stability Atlas provides detailed information about the stability of different biomes (ecosystems) on your planet. It helps you understand which areas are stable, which are at risk, and how biomes might change over time.

## Learning Objectives

### 🟢 Beginner (Ages 12-15)
- Understand what the Biome Stability Atlas shows
- Learn to read stability heatmaps
- Identify transition fronts where biomes are changing
- Understand what refugia are and why they matter

### 🟡 Intermediate (Ages 16-18)
- Understand biome stability metrics
- Interpret transition risk levels
- Use refugia overlays for conservation planning
- Queue mitigation events to protect vulnerable areas

### 🔴 Advanced (Ages 19-99)
- Understand the biome stability snapshot system
- Interpret complex biome transition models
- Use the atlas for climate impact assessment
- Understand the relationship between biome stability and other domains

## Step-by-Step Walkthrough

### Step 1: Opening the Biome Stability Atlas

1. From the main dashboard, click on **Biome Stability Atlas** in the navigation menu
2. The atlas will load with a snapshot of current biome stability
3. You'll see three main sections:
   - **Stability Heatmap** (top): Color-coded map showing biome stability
   - **Transition Analysis** (middle): Transition fronts and refugia
   - **Action Panel** (bottom): Commands and projections

### Step 2: Reading the Stability Heatmap

**🟢 Beginner:**

The stability heatmap shows how stable each biome is:

| Color | Meaning | Example |
|-------|---------|---------|
| 🟢 Dark Green | Very Stable | Ancient rainforest |
| 🟢 Light Green | Stable | Mature forest |
| 🟡 Yellow | Changing | Transition zone |
| 🟠 Orange | At Risk | Stressed ecosystem |
| 🔴 Red | Collapsing | Dying biome |

**🟡 Intermediate:**

Each biome cell shows:
- Current biome type (forest, desert, grassland, etc.)
- Stability score (0-100)
- Trend indicator (improving, stable, declining)
- Transition risk (low, medium, high)

**🔴 Advanced:**

Stability is computed from:
- Species diversity metrics
- Climate suitability
- Disturbance history
- Resilience factors

### Step 3: Understanding Transition Fronts

**🟢 Beginner:**

Transition fronts show where biomes are changing:

```
Stable Forest  ────────►  Transition Zone  ────────►  New Biome
  (Green)              (Yellow/Orange)             (New Color)
```

**🟡 Intermediate:**

Transition fronts show:
- Location (where change is happening)
- Direction (which way the biome is shifting)
- Speed (how fast the change is occurring)
- Cause (climate change, disturbance, etc.)

**🔴 Advanced:**

Transition fronts are modeled using:
- Climate envelope shifts
- Species migration rates
- Disturbance propagation
- Succession dynamics

### Step 4: Understanding Refugia

**🟢 Beginner:**

Refugia are safe havens where species can survive when conditions change:

```
Refugium:  🌳🌳🌳🌳🌳
            (Species survive here during bad times)
```

**🟢 Beginner:**

Refugia are important because:
- They protect species from extinction
- They serve as sources for recolonization
- They maintain genetic diversity
- They are critical for conservation

**🟡 Intermediate:**

Refugia overlay shows:
- Location of refugia areas
- Size and quality of each refugium
- Species that depend on them
- Vulnerability to threats

**🔴 Advanced:**

Refugia are identified by:
- Microclimate stability
- Topographic complexity
- Historical persistence
- Species richness

### Step 5: Inspecting Transition Risk

**🟢 Beginner:**

See which biomes are at risk:
1. Click on any biome cell
2. View transition risk in the inspector panel
3. See what the biome might become
4. Understand why it's changing

**🟡 Intermediate:**

Transition risk inspection shows:
- Current biome type
- Potential future biomes
- Probability of each transition
- Time to transition
- Primary drivers

**🔴 Advanced:**

Transition risk is computed from:
- Climate projections
- Species tolerance ranges
- Disturbance regimes
- Feedback loops

### Step 6: Listing Refugia

**🟢 Beginner:**

See all refugia on the planet:
1. Click **"List Refugia"**
2. View all refugia locations
3. See which species use each refugium
4. Identify most important refugia

**🟡 Intermediate:**

Refugia list shows:
- All refugia locations
- Size and quality metrics
- Species that depend on them
- Threat level

**🔴 Advanced:**

Refugia analysis includes:
- Connectivity analysis
- Climate resilience
- Species complementarity
- Conservation priority scoring

### Step 7: Queuing Mitigation Events

**🟢 Beginner:**

Protect vulnerable biomes:
1. Click on a biome at risk
2. Click **"Queue Mitigation Event"**
3. Choose a mitigation action
4. Preview the expected outcome
5. Commit the event

**🟡 Intermediate:**

Mitigation options:
- Reforestation
- Fire management
- Invasive species control
- Habitat restoration
- Climate adaptation

**🔴 Advanced:**

Mitigation events use:
- Deterministic simulation
- Multi-objective optimization
- Cost-benefit analysis
- Uncertainty quantification

## Key Concepts

### 🟢 Beginner Concepts

1. **Biome** - A large ecosystem with similar plants and animals
2. **Stability** - How likely a biome is to stay the same
3. **Transition Front** - Where one biome is changing into another
4. **Refugium** - A safe place where species can survive bad conditions

### 🟡 Intermediate Concepts

1. **Climate Envelope** - The range of climate conditions a species can tolerate
2. **Succession** - How ecosystems change over time after disturbance
3. **Resilience** - How well an ecosystem can recover from disturbance
4. **Disturbance** - Events that disrupt ecosystems (fire, storms, etc.)

### 🔴 Advanced Concepts

1. **Biome Stability Snapshot** - Complete biome state at one tick
2. **Species Migration Rate** - How fast species can move to new areas
3. **Feedback Loop** - When a change causes more of the same change
4. **Multi-objective Optimization** - Balancing multiple goals at once

## Troubleshooting

### Issue: Stability heatmap shows "Loading..."

**Solution:**
- Check if the simulation is running
- Verify biome data is computed
- Try refreshing the atlas

### Issue: Transition fronts are not visible

**Solution:**
- Check if biomes are changing
- Verify transition overlay is active
- Adjust visualization settings

### Issue: Refugia list is empty

**Solution:**
- Check if refugia have been identified
- Verify ecosystem complexity is sufficient
- Review error message for specific issue
