# Species Viewer Onboarding Guide

## Overview

The Species Viewer provides detailed information about species on your planet, including their characteristics, evolution, and survival prospects. It helps you understand how species adapt to changing conditions.

## Learning Objectives

### 🟢 Beginner (Ages 12-15)
- Understand what Species Viewer shows
- Learn to read species profiles
- Identify extinction risk levels
- Understand how species relate to each other

### 🟡 Intermediate (Ages 16-18)
- Understand species lineage graphs
- Interpret survivability radar charts
- Compare species characteristics
- Export species data for analysis

### 🔴 Advanced (Ages 19-99)
- Understand species profile snapshot system
- Interpret complex evolutionary relationships
- Use viewer for biodiversity assessment
- Understand relationship between species and other domains

## Step-by-Step Walkthrough

### Step 1: Opening the Species Viewer

1. From the main dashboard, click on **Species Viewer** in the navigation menu
2. The viewer will load with a snapshot of current species data
3. You'll see three main sections:
   - **Species List** (top): All species in your world
   - **Species Profile** (middle): Detailed information about selected species
   - **Action Panel** (bottom): Commands and tools

### Step 2: Reading Species Profiles

**🟢 Beginner:**

Each species profile shows basic information:

| Field | What It Means | Example |
|--------|----------------|----------|
| Name | Species name | "Mountain Pine" |
| Type | Kind of species | Plant, Animal, Fungi |
| Population | How many exist | 1.2 million |
| Status | How they're doing | Thriving, Stable, Declining, Endangered |

**🟡 Intermediate:**

Each species profile includes:
- Physical characteristics (size, weight, lifespan)
- Habitat preferences (temperature, moisture, altitude)
- Diet and feeding relationships
- Reproduction rate and strategy

**🔴 Advanced:**

Species profiles are computed from:
- Population dynamics models
- Genetic diversity metrics
- Environmental suitability
- Evolutionary history

### Step 3: Understanding Lineage Graphs

**🟢 Beginner:**

The lineage graph shows how species evolved:

```
Ancestor Species ──► Current Species ──► Descendant Species
      (Past)           (Present)             (Future)
```

**🟡 Intermediate:**

Lineage graphs show:
- Evolutionary relationships
- Divergence points (when species split)
- Convergence events (when unrelated species become similar)
- Extinction events (when lineages end)

**🔴 Advanced:**

Lineage is tracked using:
- Genetic similarity metrics
- Morphological changes
- Fossil record
- Molecular clock dating

### Step 4: Reading Survivability Radar

**🟢 Beginner:**

The survivability radar shows how well a species might survive:

```
        Temperature
           / \
          /   \
Diet ──●─── Habitat
          \   /
           \ /
         Climate
```

**🟢 Beginner:**

The radar shows:
- How well species handles temperature changes
- How adaptable its diet is
- How flexible its habitat needs are
- How resistant to climate change

**🟡 Intermediate:**

Survivability metrics include:
- Temperature tolerance range
- Dietary flexibility score
- Habitat adaptability index
- Climate change resilience
- Competition resistance
- Disease resistance

**🔴 Advanced:**

Survivability is calculated from:
- Species-specific tolerance ranges
- Historical survival data
- Genetic diversity metrics
- Environmental projection models

### Step 5: Understanding Extinction Risk

**🟢 Beginner:**

Extinction risk shows how likely a species is to go extinct:

| Risk Level | Color | Meaning |
|-------------|--------|---------|
| Low | Green | Species is thriving |
| Medium | Yellow | Species needs monitoring |
| High | Orange | Species is in danger |
| Critical | Red | Species may go extinct soon |

**🟡 Intermediate:**

Extinction risk factors include:
- Population size and trend
- Habitat loss
- Climate change impact
- Competition from other species
- Disease pressure

**🔴 Advanced:**

Extinction risk is computed using:
- Population viability analysis
- Habitat suitability modeling
- Climate envelope projections
- Stochastic extinction models

### Step 6: Opening Species Sheets

**🟢 Beginner:**

Get detailed information about a species:
1. Click on any species in the list
2. Click **"Open Species Sheet"**
3. View comprehensive species information
4. Explore different tabs (Biology, Habitat, Evolution)

**🟡 Intermediate:**

Species sheets include:
- Taxonomic classification
- Physical measurements
- Life cycle details
- Behavioral patterns
- Ecological relationships
- Conservation status

**🔴 Advanced:**

Species sheets provide:
- Complete taxonomic hierarchy
- Genetic information
- Physiological limits
- Niche modeling data
- Historical distribution records

### Step 7: Comparing Species

**🟢 Beginner:**

See how two species are different:
1. Select two species from the list
2. Click **"Compare Species"**
3. View side-by-side comparison
4. Note similarities and differences

**🟡 Intermediate:**

Species comparison shows:
- Physical characteristics
- Habitat requirements
- Ecological roles
- Evolutionary relationships
- Competition potential

**🔴 Advanced:**

Species comparison uses:
- Morphological similarity metrics
- Niche overlap analysis
- Genetic distance calculations
- Competition modeling

### Step 8: Exporting Species Data

**🟢 Beginner:**

Save species information for later:
1. Select one or more species
2. Click **"Export Species Packet"**
3. Choose export format (CSV, JSON)
4. Save file to your computer

**🟡 Intermediate:**

Export options include:
- Basic species information
- Complete species profiles
- Evolutionary history
- Population data
- Habitat requirements

**🔴 Advanced:**

Export formats provide:
- Structured data for analysis
- Metadata for reproducibility
- Version information
- Source citations

## Key Concepts

### 🟢 Beginner Concepts

1. **Species** - A group of organisms that can reproduce with each other
2. **Population** - How many individuals of a species exist
3. **Habitat** - The natural environment where a species lives
4. **Extinction** - When no more individuals of a species exist

### 🟡 Intermediate Concepts

1. **Lineage** - The evolutionary history of a species
2. **Survivability** - How likely a species is to survive changes
3. **Adaptation** - How species change to fit their environment
4. **Competition** - When species fight for the same resources

### 🔴 Advanced Concepts

1. **Species Profile Snapshot** - Complete species state at one tick
2. **Genetic Diversity** - How much genetic variation exists in a species
3. **Niche** - The specific role a species plays in its ecosystem
4. **Evolutionary Pressure** - Environmental factors that drive evolution

## Common Tasks

### Task 1: Check Species Status

1. Open the Species Viewer
2. Look at the species list
3. Identify species with high extinction risk (red/orange)
4. Click on at-risk species to see details
5. Consider what might help them

1. Find two species that might be related
2. Select both species
3. Click "Compare Species"
4. Look at similarities and differences
5. Check lineage graph for evolutionary relationships

2. Click "Export Species Packet"
3. Choose appropriate export format

## Troubleshooting

### Issue: Species list shows "Loading..."

**Solution:**
- Check if the simulation is running
- Verify species data is computed
- Try refreshing the viewer

### Issue: Lineage graph is not visible

**Solution:**
- Check if evolutionary data is available
- Verify species have sufficient history
- Adjust visualization settings

### Issue: Species comparison fails

**Solution:**
- Verify both species are selected
- Check if species data is complete
- Review error message for specific issue
